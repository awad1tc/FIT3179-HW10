New York City Food Week 2018

This webpage contains visulisations examining reviews of restaurants that pariticipated in New York City Food Week 2018. We will examine restaurant review scores over various dimensions by cuisine, price range and other factors. Enjoy!

New York City Restaurants by Food Review and Price Range

The following map visualises the reviews received by restaurants duing New York Food Week 2018. We can see that there is a strong concentration of restaurants in downtown Manhattan (see the focused Manhattan view for a better idea of how restaurants are positioned), with a few in Staten Island and Brooklyn. Queens and The Bronx did not have any restaurants reviewed across the data. Staten Island and Brooklyn have no expensive restaurants. Overall most restuarnts are cheap or mid priced.

Average Review Scores by Restaurant Type

This visulaisation shows the average reviews scores by category for each restaurant type against the overall average review score given to each restaurant taking into account all factors. It is interesting to see that the majority of restaurants receive category reviews on average that are worse than the overall average. For the most part price plays a factor with expensive restaurants ranking the highest.

Cheap vs. Expensive Restaurants

More expensive restaurants have higher food reviews, however this is more pronounced for American and Contemporary American cuisine. French restaurants have the smallest variance across price range. The divergence tends to get smaller as we filter by the number of reviews a restaurant has received. This indicates that better restaurants get reviewed more.

Mid Priced vs. Expensive Restaurants

For almost all cuisines review scores are closer for mid priced restaurants. For French and Mediterranean restaurants the mid priced reviews are worse than the cheap restaurants. The divergence tends to get smaller as we filter by the number of reviews a restaurant has received. This indicates that better restaurants get reviewed more, regardless of price range.

Review Score Distribution

Looking at the distribution of average review scores by both restaurant and price range we can see something interesting. Expensive restaurants never receive poor reviews, they are all distributed between 4 and 5. However, there are just as many highly rated mid priced restaurants as there are expensive ones. Cheaper restaurants have a wider variance, but still have representation between 4 and 5.

This visualisation is created by Alex Waddington. The datasource is the NYC Food Week 2018 dataset available from Kaggle.
